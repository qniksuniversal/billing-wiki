### First - creating a clan

1. Open the menu with the command <code>/clan</code> and click “Create a clan”, or use <code>/clan create</code>.
2. Enter your clan tag into the chat.
3. Enter the clan name in the chat.
4. After reporting success, the clan is created.
5. To cancel, write <code>cancel</code> or <code>cancel</code>.

### Second - how to join a clan

1. Open <code>/clan list</code> and select a clan.
2. In the clan profile, click “Join” (available only for public clans).

### Joining a clan through an invitation

1. The player who wants to invite you must write /clan invite "Your name"
2. Afterwards, an invitation to the clan will appear in your chat, to join, click “Accept”

In both cases you will find yourself in a clan.

### Clan privacy

- **public** - any player can join through a clan profile.
- **invite_only** - entry is by invitation only.
- **private** — the clan is hidden in the list, the profile is visible only to members.

Switching is available in the GUI settings or with the command <code>/clan privacy</code>.

### Invitations

- The invitation is sent by a player with the right to invite participants.
- The player receives a clickable message with “Accept” and “Decline” buttons.
- The invitation will automatically expire after some time.

### Clan Profile

Profile shows:
- tag and title;
- date of creation;
- privacy;
- description;
- list of participants (partially);
- head of the clan;
- the “Join” button for public clans, if the clan is not public there will be no button.

### Clan settings (GUI)

In the settings section the following are available:
- change of privacy;
- change the name and tag (via entering into the chat);
- description of the clan;
- banner color;
- enable/disable tag display;
- publicity of the profile;
- Friendly Fire (friendly fire).

![Clan settings](guilds/guilds4.png)

### Clan chat

- Turned on/off by the leader with the command <code>/clan chat</code>.
- Messages are sent with a prefix (usually <code>*</code>).

Example: <code>* hello clan</code>

### Roles and rights

Default roles:
- **Leader** - all rights.
- **Co-Leader** - all rights except disbandment.
- **Moderator** - invitation and kick.
- **Member**—basic member.

Role assignment:
- Can be assigned with the command <code>/clan role assign</code>;
- Can be issued through the GUI “Manage roles/list of participants”, you can assign roles to participants.

### Friendly Fire

If Friendly Fire is turned off, damage between members of the same clan is blocked (including damage from arrows).

### Quick answers to common questions

- **Why can’t I join the clan?**
perhaps the clan is full or you already have a clan.
- **Why can’t I kick a player from the clan?**
You need rights, and you cannot kick equal/higher in role.
- **Why don’t I see the clan in the list?**
Most likely the clan is private or the profile is closed to everyone.
